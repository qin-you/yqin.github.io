Research Interest

I work in the field of Reinforcement Leaning(RL), Machine Learning(ML), Recommendation System(RS), Linux Kernel and Linux drivers. I have researched the following topics:
  • RL: The training process analysis and termination condition of the training process of a Reinforcement Learning system have always been the key issues to train an RL agent. We propose Exploration Entropy to alleviate the issues to some extent.
  • HRL: In order to stabilize the low level more quickly and acceleratethe non-stationary stage in end-to-end HRL, I use a Gates module composed of several scoring machines to score each low-level policy and judge which one has the most success potential to execute a specific goal.
  • RS: In General RS process, Recall and Sort are mainly steps. It is important for recall stage to retrieving a subset of potentially relevant from a large pool of available options. To improve the performance of Recall, I learned lots of basic recall algorithms such as FM,FFM,MF,swing etc. Besides, I have attempted to use contrastive learning and multigoal trick into Dual Tower Model to increase CTR.
  • Kernel: I've learned the unix-like operating system xv6, developed by MIT, and completed all the labs, including implementing copy-on-write, mmap, parallelism and some other system calls and mechanism. Besides, I have done some work about U-Boot and kernel porting.
  • Drivers: I have implemented drivers for common peripherals on the ARM architecture, including bare-metal drivers and Linux system drivers, such as I2C, SPI, timer, platform and misc. Besides, I have also studied the driver development on the x86 architecture, such as PCI and network drivers.
